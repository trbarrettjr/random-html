Notes on creating QR Codes

For QR codes to work in the form of a "call to action" then they need to have a protocol associated with them. An example of a protocol is https://. This tells the smartphone that it will populate a website address. Below are some examples of protocols to utilize on a phone.

Source: https://developer.apple.com/library/archive/featuredarticles/iPhoneURLScheme_Reference/Introduction/Introduction.html

Protocol Examples

Email using an email application – mailto:example.user@example.com

Telephone Call – tel:1-208-555-1212

SMS or Text Messaging – sms:1-208-555-1212

Website – https://www.example.com

Website Strategies

Due to the "static" nature of QR codes, it is impossible to change the QR code after it has been generated. Therefore it is recommended that a URL forwarding service be utilized to create "dynamic" weblinks. An example of this service is bit.ly.

Data Source Encoding

Other data types, such as vCARDS can be encoded into a QR code. Most phones will recognize the format will ask the user to create a contact. It does not automatically place the data in the user's contact list.
